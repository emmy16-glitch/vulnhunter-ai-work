# UI Acceptance Criteria

A future implementation is acceptable only when:

1. Unauthorized routes and actions are rejected by the backend.
2. A scan cannot be submitted without a valid authorization reference.
3. Scope, DNS, pinning, budget, and cancellation states are visible.
4. Findings preserve redaction and evidence provenance.
5. Reviewer decisions remain independent and adjudication is role-separated.
6. Release blockers are explicit and cannot be bypassed.
7. Dataset readiness reports provenance, duplicates, balance, diversity, disputes,
   and leakage risk.
8. Every loading, empty, failure, blocked, and permission-denied state is designed.
9. Desktop, tablet, and mobile layouts pass the same critical workflows.
10. Keyboard, focus, contrast, labels, and screen-reader announcements are tested.
11. Design tokens and component variants match the approved Figma library.
12. No connector or model-training control is exposed before its governed milestone.
